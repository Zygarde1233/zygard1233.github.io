3D Biot-Savart Law: current loops and field vectors

Equation

\[ \mathrm{d}\vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I\,\mathrm{d}\vec{\ell}\times\hat{r}}{r^2} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I\,\mathrm{d}\vec{\ell}\times\vec{r}}{r^3} \]

The orange segment is one \(\mathrm{d}\vec{\ell}\). The purple vector is its contribution \(\mathrm{d}\vec{B}\). The red vector is the sum from all path segments.

What the 3D view teaches

\(\mathrm{d}\vec{\ell}\)A tiny directed piece of the current path. The highlighted orange segment is the one currently being inspected.
\(\vec{r}\) and \(\hat{r}\)\(\vec{r}\) points from that source segment to the blue probe. \(\hat{r}\) keeps only that direction.
\(\mathrm{d}\vec{\ell}\times\hat{r}\)The cross product makes \(\mathrm{d}\vec{B}\) perpendicular to both the current element and the source-to-probe direction.
\(N\) slicesSmall \(N\) gives a rough sum. Increasing \(N\) makes each \(\mathrm{d}\vec{\ell}\) smaller and approaches the line integral.
\(\sum \mathrm{d}\vec{B}\)The full field at the probe comes from adding contributions from every segment around the loop.
